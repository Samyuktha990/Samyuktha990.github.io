About

A broad hardware profile with a common thread: reliable systems.

My background combines semiconductor testing, design verification, physical design, and Python-based automation. I started in RF subsystem validation, moved into SystemVerilog/UVM-based verification of AMBA protocols, and now continue building experience across digital design flows, FPGA-based experimentation, and EDA workflows.

Through my M.Sc. at Universität Bremen, I have worked with RTL-to-GDSII implementation, RISC-V based SoC design, cleanroom semiconductor fabrication, and measurement automation using Red Pitaya platforms.

I am exploring roles across IC verification, digital design, VLSI/physical design, EDA automation, and data-driven hardware workflows. Across these areas, my interest is the same: understanding hardware behavior, debugging complex systems, and using automation to make engineering workflows more reliable.

What connects my interests?

Verification, VLSI, physical design, embedded/FPGA workflows, and AI/ML may look different on paper, but they share a practical engineering foundation: modeling system behavior, testing assumptions, analyzing data, and improving reliability through structured debugging and automation.
